Projects

Principal Investigator: Charles L. Brooks III
University of Michigan, Department of Chemistry, Department of Biophysics

Nov 2022-Jun 2024

Atomic environment vectors (AEVs) are descriptors that quantify chemical environments of a molecule at the scale of an atom. There are many ways to approach this, including the focus of my Master's student research project - AEVs based on atom-centered symmetry functions (ACSFs), as proposed by Behler and Perrinello and implemented by TorchANI. These AEVs are invariant to rotation, translation, and permutation of atoms. We are interested in learning how this type of AEV encodes information in latent space, and whether it has the capability to categorize unique atomic environments.

  • Compiled dataset by extracting and parsing molecules used in CGenFF validation set
  • Performed agglomerative, KMeans and DBSCAN clustering on AEVs
  • Analyzed cluster membership and evaluated AEVs as predictors for chemical identity as annotated by CGenFF atom typing

Results: ACSF-based AEVs are informative and demonstrated consistent atom type assignments with human chemists' manual annotations for CHARMM.

Jun 2024-Jun 2025

As a post-Master's practical training student, I continued my work in investigating atomic environment descriptors. My next objective was to inform and improve molecular simulation accuracy using insights from ACSF-based AEVs.

  • Set up a machine learning workflow for training and optimizing atomic environment vectors (AEV) generation parameters for accurate atom type labels
  • Visualized AEVs in machine learning latent space via PCA and t-SNE
  • Investigated the impact of differentially weighted radial and angular sub-vectors

Principal Investigator: David R. Koes
University of Pittsburgh, Department of Computational and Systems Biology

Grid-based inhomogenous solvation theory (GIST) can quantify thermodynamic information and molecule distribution at considerable accuracy. This information has the potential to be exploited in structure-based drug discovery to determine chemical features on a pharmacophore, which is my objective for this project: to evaluate feature prediction accuracy of a machine learning model using GIST data.

  • Compiled a dataset using grid-based inhomogeneous solvation theory (GIST) based energy calculation on PDBBind protein simulation data
  • Developed a convolutional neural network (CNN) model using Pytorch for pharmacophore feature prediction

Results: Best models across pharmacophore features reached 0.6 AUC or better despite feature rarity limitation.
